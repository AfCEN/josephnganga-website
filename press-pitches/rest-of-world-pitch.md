**The deeper story:**

AfCEN's intelligence platform tracks energy projects, strategic minerals, agricultural supply chains, and regulatory environments across 54 African markets. The same continent that holds 70% of global cobalt, large shares of lithium, tantalum, rare earths, and platinum group metals — the inputs for every advanced chip, weapons system, and AI accelerator being built right now.

China spent 20 years quietly building the relationships and off-take agreements to control access to those minerals. The United States and Europe are scrambling to catch up through the Minerals Security Partnership, the Global Gateway, and Development Finance Corporation investments.

Joseph Ng'ang'a is building the intelligence infrastructure that sits in the middle of that competition — using AI systems trained to monitor exactly the kind of on-the-ground data that no satellite or Bloomberg terminal can capture. That makes AfCEN less a climate finance company and more a piece of contested geopolitical infrastructure.
